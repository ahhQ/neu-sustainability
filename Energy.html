GHG EMISSIONS TRENDS

Reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions is another key aspect of our energy saving effort.

Tracking our GHG emissions over time is essential to understanding our progress in reducing our carbon footprint. The chart shows the total GHG emissions in metric tons of CO2 equivalent (MT eCO2) from 2005 to 2022:

Continued Reduction

We've seen a general decrease in GHG emissions, dropping from 76,370 MT eCO2 in 2005 to 54,640 in 2022. Reflecting our continuous efforts to implement efficient energy practices and reduce our carbon footprint.

BUILDING PERFORMANCE

Examining the GHG emissions by building allows us to pinpoint where we can make the most significant improvements. The chart compares our buildings' performance against Boston's Building Emissions Reduction and Disclosure Ordinance (BERDO) 2030 goals:

    Progress Towards Goals: The chart shows how individual buildings are meeting or exceeding the BERDO 2030 goals, with most buildings already achieving significant reductions in emissions.

    Targeted Improvements: Identifying buildings that lag behind the goals allows us to prioritize upgrades and retrofits, ensuring all buildings contribute to our overall emissions reduction targets.

Join Our Sustainable Energy Initiatives

By understanding and optimizing our energy consumption, Northeastern University is advancing towards a more sustainable campus. Achieving our goals relies on the collective efforts of our entire community.

Together, we can reduce our energy footprint and foster a greener, more sustainable future.

Energy Conservation

Implement simple habits to save energy, practice energy conservation in our everyday life

Renewable Energy

Advocate for and support the use of renewable energy sources including but not limited to solar and wind.

Energy Efficiency

Use energy-efficient appliances and methods to reduce consumption.

Energy Monitoring

Stay informed about your energy use and participate in campus initiatives to track and reduce overall energy consumption.
