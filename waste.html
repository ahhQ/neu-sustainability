Waste Management Trend

Northeastern University is dedicated to sustainability through effective waste management. Over the past few years, significant progress has been made in reducing waste disposal and increasing waste diversion.

0%

INCREASE

in composting from 2019 to 2022



From 2019 to 2022, we have reduced the percentage of waste disposed from 59% to 52%. During this period, our recycling efforts saw a slight decrease in percentage but remained strong, while composting increased significantly from 19% to 27%. The introduction of donation and sale in 2021 also contributed to our overall waste diversion efforts.

In terms of total waste, the amount disposed dropped from 3,352 tons in 2019 to 2,371 tons in 2022.Recycling and composting showed fluctuations due to the pandemic but ended with an increase in both categories. Donations and sales, starting from zero, reached 7 tons by the end of 2022.

Our ongoing commitment to sustainability has led to substantial improvements in waste management, highlighting our dedication to reducing environmental impact and promoting a greener campus.

SINGLE-STREAM RECYCLING
SINGLE-STREAM RECYCLING

has been a key component of Northeastern University's waste management strategy. This method allows
for the collection of various recyclable materials — such as cardboard, paper, and bottles/cans — in a single
bin, simplifying the recycling process and encouraging higher participation rates.

Our ongoing commitment to sustainability is evident through the success of our single-stream recycling
program. These efforts have not only improved waste management practices but also fostered a culture
of sustainability at Northeastern, demonstrating our dedication to reducing environmental impact and
promoting greener practices campus-wide.

Looking at the 2022 calender year, higher food waste is observed during the academic months when students are on campus. For instance, the months of January to April and again from September to early December show significant spikes in food waste. These peaks align with the semesters when the majority of students are present, leading to increased dining hall activity and food consumption.

Noticeably lower food waste levels occur during the summer months (June, July and August) and during breaks such as the winter recess (late December). These periods coincide with times when fewer students are present, resulting in reduced food service and consequently, less waste.

To further enhance our understanding of food waste management, it's crucial to examine where the majority of food waste is generated across campus. The following treemap illustrates food waste by location, highlighting the specific areas that contribute the most to our overall food waste.

The treemap highlights significant food waste in key dining service areas such as the Stetson Hall West and International Village (INV) loading docks, as well as Curry Student Center and Marino Recreation Center. Understanding this distribution enables us to target the most impactful areas, effectively reducing food waste and promoting sustainability on campus.

Moving forward, Northeastern University will implement advanced food waste tracking and reduction technologies in these critical areas. Collaborating with dining services, we aim to explore innovative solutions such as composting programs, food donation partnerships, and improved inventory management. These efforts will strengthen our commitment to sustainability and set a benchmark for other institutions.
